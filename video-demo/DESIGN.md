# Cours d'Arabe Brand Reference

## Overview

Cours d'Arabe uses a dark, information-dense learning dashboard with a compact identity header, search control, summary metrics, and a six-column RTL course grid. Deep blue-black surfaces support high-contrast French interface text and prominent Arabic course titles. Gold marks the project identity, while green, violet, and orange distinguish Nahw, Sarf, and Balagha. The overall feel is scholarly, structured, restrained, and modern.

## Colors

- **Canvas**: `#06090F` — deepest full-frame background.
- **Primary Surface**: `#0C1119` — main page background.
- **Raised Surface**: `#131B28` — cards and elevated panels.
- **Primary Text**: `#E2E8F0` — interface headings and core copy.
- **Arabic Ink**: `#F8FAFC` — Arabic course titles and highest-emphasis text.
- **Secondary Text**: `#94A3B8` — subtitles, metadata, and supporting copy.
- **Gold Accent**: `#F0B429` — brand identity, focus, and key highlights.
- **Gold Hover**: `#F7C948` — brighter gold emphasis.
- **Nahw**: `#34D399` — grammar category markers and badges.
- **Sarf**: `#A78BFA` — morphology category markers and badges.
- **Balagha**: `#FB923C` — rhetoric category markers and badges.

## Typography

- **Interface family**: Inter, weights 400, 500, 600, 700, and 800. Use for English/French overlays, labels, metrics, and controls.
- **Arabic family**: Noto Naskh Arabic, weights 400, 600, and 700. Use for Arabic identity, course names, and authentic interface content.
- **Primary hierarchy**: Inter 800 for major Latin headlines; captured page H1 is 32px.
- **Secondary hierarchy**: Inter 500–600 for section labels and metadata; captured page H2 is 18px.
- **Arabic hierarchy**: Noto Naskh Arabic 400–700 with generous line height; captured course headings are 26px.
- **Numerals**: Inter with tabular numerals for course, lesson, duration, and progress metrics.

## Elevation

Depth comes from restrained surface shifts, translucent white glass, thin low-opacity borders, and dark layered shadows rather than bright panels. Cards use rounded corners from 12px to 16px, subtle borders, and compact shadows; focused elements may add a localized `#F0B429` glow. Keep background depth tonal across `#06090F`, `#0C1119`, and `#131B28`, with faint gold or violet radial illumination only behind important content.

## Components

- **Identity Header**: Arabic teacher name above the bold Cours d'Arabe wordmark, paired with a muted section subtitle.
- **Metric Pill Cluster**: compact rounded pills for catalogue size, lesson volume, duration, and completion state.
- **Search Field**: wide dark input with a thin border, 12px radius, muted label, and subdued placeholder.
- **RTL Course Catalogue**: six-column grid of dense course cards; Arabic titles dominate, while Latin metadata stays compact.
- **Discipline Badges**: rounded uppercase Nahw, Sarf, and Balagha chips using their semantic colors.
- **Course Progress Cards**: 16px-radius raised surfaces containing lesson/module counts, Arabic summary, thin progress rule, duration, word count, and resources.
- **Theme Control**: small fixed circular moon/sun control with low-contrast border and gold icon.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's

- Keep large areas on `#06090F` or `#0C1119`; reserve `#131B28` for raised content.
- Preserve readable Arabic with Noto Naskh Arabic and right-to-left alignment.
- Use `#F0B429` sparingly for identity, focus lines, and decisive emphasis.
- Preserve semantic category mapping: Nahw `#34D399`, Sarf `#A78BFA`, Balagha `#FB923C`.
- Use thin borders, soft glass, rounded corners, compact metadata, and generous outer margins.

### Don'ts

- Do not introduce colors outside the captured palette.
- Do not replace authentic Arabic content with fabricated text or generic imagery.
- Do not use bright full-frame backgrounds, heavy gradients, or glossy 3D treatments.
- Do not use decorative Latin typefaces; Inter remains the interface voice.
- Do not flatten every element onto one plane; retain subtle surface and border hierarchy.
